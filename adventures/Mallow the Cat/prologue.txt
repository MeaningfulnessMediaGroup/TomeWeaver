The Hawthorne Estate. To the human eye, it was a sprawling Victorian mansion filled with drafty corridors, slightly eccentric wallpaper, and an overgrown garden that had long since forgotten the touch of a lawnmower. To Mallow, however, it was a sovereign kingdom, and he was its absolute, albeit frequently horizontal, monarch.

Being a ragdoll cat of such marshmallow-white fluffiness is an exhausting vocation. It requires a level of judgmental staring, strategic shedding on dark fabrics, and the maintenance of a very specific, slightly-overweight physique that would break a lesser creature. The weight of crown is heavy, but the weight of a well-fed ragdoll is heavier.

Fourteen hours ago, Mallow had made a decisive executive command: the world was to be ignored. He had claimed the Forbidden Velvet Armchair—an emerald-green relic that Mrs. Hawthorne guarded with a zeal bordering on the religious. To her, it was a family heirloom. To Mallow, it was the only surface in the house that truly understood the contours of his majestic spine.

As the dust motes danced in the fading afternoon light, Mallow drifted through the soft, silent world of dream-mice and infinite salmon-flavored treats. He was a creature of pure, unadulterated peace. The world outside his velvet kingdom continued its frantic, pointless spinning, but for Mallow, time was merely a suggestion. A suggestion he intended to ignore until his internal clock signaled the absolute necessity of a snack.
