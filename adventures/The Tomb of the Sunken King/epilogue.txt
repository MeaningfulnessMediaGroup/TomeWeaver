Kaelen reached the open air as the tomb gave one final groan behind him.

Stone split. Water roared upward through cracks in the ancient floor. Somewhere below, the flooded throne room collapsed into itself, dragging the last echoes of the Sunken King down into the dark that had claimed his kingdom centuries before. The ruin shuddered once, twice, then settled into silence.

The curse did not scream.

It simply ended.

For a long moment, Kaelen remained beneath the bruised sky, the Golden Crown clutched against his chest, its cold metal no longer burning with dead sunlight. Around him, the overgrown ruins seemed smaller than before. The vines stirred in the night wind. Insects began their thin, ordinary music. Far off, some unseen bird called from the marsh, as if announcing that death had lost its claim here.

Kaelen looked back at the sealed wound of the tomb entrance.

He thought of the bones in the whispering hall, the flooded throne, the crown upon the skull of a king who had mistaken possession for eternity. All that gold. All that power. All those spells and sacrifices and buried servants.

In the end, the tomb had kept everything.

Except him.

Kaelen gave a dry, exhausted laugh and turned away.

The crown would buy freedom, perhaps. Or enemies. Or a ship out of Veyr. Or three months of food and a better dagger. Treasure always promised a future, but never explained the cost of reaching it.

Still, dawn was coming.

A thin blade of gold appeared along the eastern marshes, touching the broken stones with pale fire. For the first time since he had entered the ruins, nothing glowed in warning. No whisper rose behind him. No ancient monarch called his name.

Kaelen adjusted the weight of his pack and stepped onto the old road.

Behind him lay the Tomb of the Sunken King, drowned at last.

Ahead lay whatever he was clever enough to survive.
