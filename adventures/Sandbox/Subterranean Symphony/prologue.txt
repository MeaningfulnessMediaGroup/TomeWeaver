In the year 2634, humanity exists far below Earth's toxic surface in a labyrinthine network of cities carved into stone and sea floor. Neo-Atlantis pulses with energy as millions navigate its gleaming streets beneath crystal-domed skies that mirror the ocean above.

Zeta Vox stretches awake, her mind still lingering on dreams of forgotten tunnels winding deep into the planet's heart. She squints at the flickering lights casting wavering shadows across her small apartment, a symptom of the crystal node failures plaguing the city. The nodes, which draw power from Earth's core and distribute it through glowing conduits that crisscross every tunnel and ocean floor settlement, have been faltering with increasing frequency.

Neo-Atlantis is a marvel of engineering: towering aqueducts channel water from deep-sea geothermal vents to hydroelectric turbines. High-speed trains dart through transparent tubes above streets where pedestrians walk beneath canopies of bioluminescent moss. But the city's heart is failing, and Zeta fears what this means for their tenuous existence. As a junior engineer at the node maintenance facility, she knows how quickly power loss could turn their utopian society into a dystopian nightmare.

She dresses in her standard black jumpsuit embossed with the neon blue logo of the Engineering Guild and grabs her tool kit, determined to find answers beneath the waves.
