The neon lights of Sector 4 pierced through the rain-streaked window, casting an eerie glow across a scene straight out of a nightmare. The cramped motel room reeked of sweat, stimulants, and death - an all-too-familiar aroma to Jax's seasoned nostrils

He lay sprawled on the floor beside a lifeless body that was all too familiar: Marcus Kaine, VP of Tech Operations for NEXUS Corp. The man who'd hired Jax just days ago for... what? His memories were as fragmented as his malfunctioning cyber-implants flickering in the darkness

Jax's hand instinctively went to his side, where he felt a familiar weight - cold steel. A .45-caliber pistol, its barrel still warm with recent use. The same kind of gun that had left Kaine dead at Jax's feet, a single bullet hole between the eyes

With effort, Jax sat up, fighting through the pounding in his head. His vision swam, overloading with glitching holographic displays and error messages from his damaged neural implant. He shook them off like buzzing flies, focusing on the here and now

Scanning the room for clues, he spotted a gaping hole in one wall where someone had pried back a floorboard. There, nestled among the cheap wood fibers, was a sleek data drive, its surface unmarred except for a few deep scratches. Jax knew that look - this wasn't your run-of-the-mill storage device

He plucked it from its hiding spot and turned it over in his hands. Encryption keys danced across the screen as he examined it, flickering like ghosts in the rain-streaked light. This was what Kaine had hired him for... but to do what? And why did Jax end up with a smoking gun and no memory?
