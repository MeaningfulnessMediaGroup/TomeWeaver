A chilling wind howls through Evernight's narrow streets as Kaelen crouches atop a crumbling ledge, his shadow-cloak billowing around him like smoke from some extinguished flame. He is still and silent as stone, save for the faint rise and fall of his chest - an unobtrusive beat in the symphony of the city's restless slumber.

Below, flickering torchlight spills from the windows of the Red Raven Inn, casting dancing shadows across cobblestones slick with rain. The patrons' laughter echoes hollowly through the night air, their revelry as fleeting and insubstantial as mist at dawn. Kaelen watches them for a time, his eyes narrowed to slits as he picks out faces familiar to him: thugs, cutthroats, and scoundrels of every stripe. All vying for their own share of the city's dark bounty.

His gaze lingers on one particular figure, seated alone in a shadowed corner - a woman clad in robes the color of night, her face obscured by the hood pulled low over her brow. She sips from an ornate chalice with measured grace, each movement deliberate and precise as a dancer's pirouette. Kaelen knows this is no ordinary patron: she bears the mark of one who traffics in secrets, whispers traded for coin or favors rendered in the dark.

A shiver runs down his spine as he remembers her words from their last meeting: "The Crown of Whispers grows restless, master thief. Its power wanes, and soon... soon even its prison may crumble." She had spoken then of a curse, ancient and insidious - one that would consume the artifact and unleash an army of spectral horrors upon the world if it were not retrieved in time.

Kaelen exhales slowly, watching his breath mist in the cold air before dissipating into nothingness. His fingers trace the edges of the lockpicks nestled within his belt pouch - starlight-crafted and sharp as a winter's kiss. He knows that what awaits him within Wraiths' Tomb is no mere challenge, but a descent into madness itself. And yet, he cannot deny the allure of such a prize, or the whispering promise of his own legend growing ever brighter.

With one last glance at the woman in the Red Raven's shadows, Kaelen pushes off from the ledge and disappears into the night, a fleeting specter bound for the jaws of fate.
