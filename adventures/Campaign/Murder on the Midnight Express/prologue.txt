The Midnight Express sliced through the crisp Alpine air like a gleaming blade as it wound its way through the snow-covered peaks. Inside the opulent first-class dining car, passengers nibbled on caviar and sipped vintage champagne under crystal chandeliers, blissfully unaware of the storm brewing in their midst. The rhythmic clacking of wheels against track lulled them into a false sense of security. But for one man - Baron von Schtupelpumper - this night would end far more dramatically than any could have imagined. As dinner concluded and guests retired to the lounge car for brandy, an eerie silence fell over the dining area. It was then that Arthur Pendelton emerged from his private compartment to find the Baron slumped over in his seat, a half-eaten dessert congealing on the table before him - and a look of horror frozen on his face. The retired detective's mind immediately began to race with possibilities as he noticed the small glass vial tucked into the Baron's pocket, its contents suspiciously dark and viscous.
