As dawn broke over the majestic Alps, Arthur Pendelton sat alone in the now silent lounge car, his pipe smoldering gently as he reflected on the night's events. The killer had been unmasked, justice served amidst the chaos of the train's final stop. He allowed himself a moment to appreciate the beauty of the snow-capped peaks outside before turning his attention inward once more. Deep down, Pendelton knew that this would not be the last mystery he'd solve aboard these elegant machines - there was something about the confines of first class, the whirring of wheels on track, that seemed to bring out both the best and worst in people. He was, after all, only human himself.
